The following table presents the verb forms as given in Hoch as I understand them.
An empty cell does not mean that no such form exists, just that no information is given.
A dash (-) means that the form does not exist.
A star (*) indicates that the form is not found in Hoch but I find evidence from other sources that this form exists.
Upper case letters are wild cards for the root consonants. They should be replaced by the actual MdC transliteration values which appear in the actual verb.
Lower case letters are MdC transliteration values
The final root letters in i and w and y and t are "weak consonants" and may or may not be present in the writing.
Other forms like .n, .tw, etc. must be present, although, again, final w and t may be absent in writing.
Doubling forms (e.g. ABB) and weak forms (ending in i,y, or w) are often referred to as "mutable verbs".

For the weak verbs (those ending in i or w or y) I include the weak vowel even if it is never attested in any text and/or never used in any transliteration.
A dash (-) indicates that the form does not exist or is unattested. A blank space means no information is available.
In the "NAME" column, the number following the sign are the section numbers in Hoch where the relevant information can be found.
The forms are usually given in the order of their presentation in Hoch.
